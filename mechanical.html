Mechanical Design

Calvin's physical structure — the chassis that holds everything together and the drive system that keeps it moving.

01 Chassis

Structure

Calvin's chassis is a custom-designed frame sized to accommodate the Arduino GIGA R1, ODrive S1 controllers, battery, and motor mounts in a compact vertical stack. The layout keeps the center of mass as low as possible to reduce the torque demand on the balance loop.

  • Two-wheeled self-balancing form factor
  • Vertical electronics stack — Jetson on top, GIGA R1 mid, battery low
  • Motor mounts integrated into the base plate
  • Removable side panels for cable access

Key Dimensions

02 Drive System

Motors

D5312S brushless outrunner motors — the same class of motor used in larger multirotor drones. Running them direct-drive (no gearbox) keeps the drivetrain simple and eliminates backlash, which matters for a balance control loop.

  • 53 mm stator diameter
  • ~170 KV rating
  • Peak torque ~1.5 N·m
  • Direct drive — no gearbox
  • One per side, mirrored mount

Wheels

Wheels mount directly to the motor shaft hub. The wheel diameter determines the linear speed-to-RPM ratio and affects the balance controller's effective gain — larger diameter means more ground speed per unit of motor velocity.

  • Hub-mounted to motor shaft
  • Pneumatic or solid rubber tread
  • Matched pair — identical left and right

Motor Controllers

Each motor is driven by a dedicated ODrive S1 single-axis brushless controller. The ODrives run field-oriented control (FOC) internally, accepting high-level velocity setpoints from the GIGA R1 over CAN bus.

  • ODrive S1 × 2
  • FOC — smooth, efficient torque
  • Velocity control mode
  • CAN bus · 250 kbps
  • Up to 56 V / 40 A per controller

Direct-Drive Topology

Running the D5312S motors direct-drive means the wheel speed equals the motor speed with no intermediate gearing. This simplifies the mechanical design and removes any backlash or compliance that would degrade the balance loop's response. The trade-off is that the motor must produce all required torque without mechanical advantage — the D5312S's relatively high peak torque for its size makes this feasible at the wheel diameters chosen.

03 Power

Battery

Calvin runs on a LiPo battery pack chosen to sit within the ODrive S1's voltage range while delivering enough current for two motors under aggressive balance corrections. The pack mounts at the base of the chassis to keep the center of mass low.

Chemistry LiPo
Nominal voltage 6S · 22.2 V
ODrive input range 8–56 V (within spec)
Placement Base of chassis — low CoM

Power Distribution

The battery feeds the ODrive S1 controllers directly at pack voltage. Lower-voltage rails for the Arduino GIGA R1 and Jetson Orin Nano are derived via dedicated BEC / DC-DC converters to isolate logic power from the noisy motor supply.

  • Motor rail Battery voltage direct to both ODrive S1 controllers
  • 5 V logic DC-DC step-down for Arduino GIGA R1 and peripheral sensors
  • Jetson Regulated supply via barrel jack — Jetson Orin Nano carrier board input
